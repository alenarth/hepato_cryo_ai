1. Random Forest Algorithm Performance

Model Fit (Actual vs. Predicted)

Scatter plot showing the correlation between observed in vitro viability and AI-predicted viability.

Interpretation: The closeness of the points to the diagonal reference line demonstrates the model's high coefficient of determination (R² = 0.979), confirming that the AI generalizes well and accurately predicts hepatocyte survival with a minimal error margin (RMSE = 4.99%).

Survival Zones (Heatmap)

Predictive mapping of all possible interactions between DMSO and trehalose concentrations.

Interpretation: The lighter areas indicate the optimal cryopreservation points discovered by the AI. The toxicity threshold is visually apparent, where excessive cryoprotectant concentrations lead to a sharp drop in estimated cell viability.

Learning Curve

Model performance as a function of training set size, assessing generalization capacity.

Interpretation: Converging training and validation error curves indicate the model is not overfitting and that the dataset size is sufficient for reliable generalization. A small gap between the curves confirms consistent performance across different data splits.

Residuals Analysis

Distribution and structure of prediction errors across the range of predicted values.

Interpretation: Residuals scattered randomly around zero, with no systematic pattern, confirm the model has no significant bias and that the linear assumptions of the error structure are satisfied across the full range of viability predictions.

Feature Importance

Relative contribution of each cryoprotectant to the Random Forest predictions.

Interpretation: The relative importance scores reveal which cryoprotectant (DMSO or trehalose) drives the model's viability predictions most strongly. Higher importance indicates greater sensitivity of cell survival to changes in that variable's concentration.

Figure 1: Effect of DMSO Alone

Interpretation: Viability of hepatocytes frozen with increasing DMSO concentrations. HepG2 cells were frozen with 0–100% DMSO, thawed after 7 days, and viability assessed via trypan blue exclusion and MTT assays. Data represent mean ± SD from 6–15 samples per concentration (n=1–3 independent experiments). Statistical analysis used two-way ANOVA with Sidak post-test. Asterisks denote p<0.0001; "ns" indicates non-significant.

Figure 2: Effect of Trehalose Alone

Interpretation: Viability of hepatocytes frozen with increasing trehalose concentrations. HepG2 cells were frozen with 20–100% trehalose, thawed after 7 days, and viability assessed using trypan blue exclusion and MTT. Data show mean ± SD from 4–5 samples per concentration (n=1–2 independent experiments).

Figure 3: Combined Effect (DMSO + Trehalose)

Interpretation: Viability of hepatocytes frozen with increasing combinations of dimethyl sulfoxide and trehalose. HepG2 cells were frozen with 5% or 10% DMSO (D) and 5–30% trehalose (T). After 7 days, cells were thawed and viability was measured by trypan blue exclusion and MTT assays. Values represent mean ± SD from 2–7 samples per trehalose concentration (n=1 independent experiment).
